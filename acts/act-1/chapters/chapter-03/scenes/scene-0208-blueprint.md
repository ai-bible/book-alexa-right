## SCENE OVERVIEW

### Core Purpose
Alexa uses deductive reasoning to understand the significance of the cat from her real memories. This scene transitions her from discovery (scene 0207) to action - she realizes the cat is living proof of Reginald's research and decides she must retrieve it.

### Scene Structure: REACTIVE
1. **Reaction**: Processing the discrepancy she found (cat present/absent)
2. **Dilemma**: What does this mean? Why was the cat removed from false memory?
3. **Decision**: Must retrieve the cat - it's evidence and redemption

### Emotional Arc
**Opening**: Focused curiosity, detective mode
**Development**: Growing excitement as logic unfolds
**Climax**: Breakthrough realization - the cat is proof
**Closing**: Determined resolve, planning next move

### Connections
**From Previous Scene (0207)**: Alexa discovered the cat discrepancy - silver-gray cat with green eyes present in real memory, absent in false one
**To Next Scene (0209)**: Alexa returns to Golden Terrace to break into Havenford's apartment and retrieve the cat

## BEAT BREAKDOWN

### Beat 1: Opening - The Anomaly (Est. 150-200 words)
**Type**: Reaction/Setup
**Purpose**: Establish Alexa's detective mindset, ground the deductive process

**Action**:
Alexa sits in her apartment (late night/early morning), reviewing her notes from the memory comparison. The cat detail stands out - too specific to be random. Silver-gray fur, green eyes, present in the lab during experiments.

**Internal Monologue Focus**:
- Why would someone remove a cat from a false memory?
- The specificity bothers her - it's not vague, it's detailed
- Small details often matter most in investigations

**Mood/Tone**: Focused, methodical, detective at work

**Sensory Details**:
- Cool blue glow of her data screen
- Quiet apartment (contrast to mental noise)
- Perhaps coffee going cold as she works

**Physical State**:
- Exhaustion ignored
- Hunched over notes/screen
- Mind fully engaged despite body's fatigue

**Ends With**: She starts laying out the logic systematically - if X, then Y...

---

### Beat 2: First Logical Step - False vs Real (Est. 150-200 words)
**Type**: Analysis
**Purpose**: Establish the baseline comparison

**Action**:
Alexa explicitly compares the two memories side by side in her mind, focusing specifically on the cat's presence/absence.

**Deductive Chain - Step 1**:
```
FALSE MEMORY (hedonist Reginald):
- No laboratory
- No research
- No cat
- Luxurious apartment, parties, indulgence

REAL MEMORY (researcher Reginald):
- Laboratory equipment
- Experimental protocols
- Cat present, participating in experiments
- Silver-gray, green eyes, specific details
```

**Internal Monologue**:
"In the false version, there's no cat because there's no laboratory, no research to hide. In the real version, the cat was there - I watched it move through the equipment, saw Reginald check its vitals after exposure to temporal fields. The corporation gave me a memory of a man who cared about nothing but pleasure, so naturally that man wouldn't have a research animal."

**Revelation Building**: The absence is intentional, not accidental

**Ends With**: But why specifically remove the cat? Why not just show a different lab or different experiments?

---

### Beat 3: Second Logical Step - Why Remove? (Est. 150-200 words)
**Type**: Deduction
**Purpose**: Move from observation to interpretation

**Action**:
Alexa pushes deeper into the question. The corporation's false memory was carefully constructed - every detail served a purpose. So every omission also serves a purpose.

**Deductive Chain - Step 2**:
```
QUESTION: Why remove the cat specifically?

HYPOTHESIS 1: Random detail omission
- REJECTED: False memory too carefully crafted
- Every detail intentional

HYPOTHESIS 2: Cats don't fit hedonist narrative
- WEAK: Many wealthy hedonists have pets
- Not sufficient reason

HYPOTHESIS 3: Cat is evidence
- The cat proves something they want hidden
- What does a research animal prove?
```

**Internal Monologue**:
"They didn't just remove research equipment or data - those are easy to dispose of, to hide. They removed a living creature. A living creature that would have been exposed to whatever Reginald was working on. A living creature that might carry physical evidence in its biology, its cells, its temporal signature."

**Tension Building**: The logic is leading somewhere specific

**Key Insight**: The cat isn't just associated with research - the cat IS evidence

**Ends With**: The cat is living proof that Reginald conducted experiments on temporal field exposure

---

### Beat 4: Third Logical Step - The Proof (Est. 150-250 words)
**Type**: Breakthrough
**Purpose**: The central revelation of the scene

**Action**:
The full deductive chain clicks into place. Alexa sees it clearly now.

**Deductive Chain - Step 3 (Complete)**:
```
PREMISE A: Corporation wants to hide Reginald's research
PREMISE B: Corporation created false memory (hedonist)
PREMISE C: False memory specifically omits the cat
PREMISE D: Real memory includes cat in experiments

CONCLUSION: The cat is physical evidence of the research

COROLLARY: The cat is still alive (or was, recently)
- If cat had died, no need to hide it
- Living cat = testable evidence
- Living cat = proof that can be examined
```

**Internal Monologue** (building to climax):
"If they wanted to hide the research completely, they could have killed the cat - one more casualty in whatever cleanup operation they ran after Reginald died. But they didn't. Or couldn't. Which means the cat exists, somewhere in the real world, carrying evidence in its cells and its temporal markers. A cat that's been exposed to experimental temporal fields wouldn't be biological normal. There would be traces, anomalies, proof that someone could test and verify. The corporation erased the cat from my memory because the cat is proof - living, breathing, undeniable proof that Reginald Havenford was conducting temporal research on living subjects."

**Emotional Peak**: Triumph of logic, first real leverage against the corporation

**Physical Reaction**:
- Pulse quickens
- Leans back from screen
- Moment of clarity, satisfaction

**Ends With**: But where is the cat now?

---

### Beat 5: Memory Recall - The Address (Est. 100-150 words)
**Type**: Solution Emergence
**Purpose**: Provide actionable next step

**Action**:
Alexa digs into the real memory more carefully. Reginald's apartment in Golden Terrace. She remembers it.

**Memory Details** (vivid sensory recall):
- 890th level, Golden Terrace
- Luxury tower, premium security
- Specific apartment: #89047
- Interior glimpses: laboratory space hidden behind elegant facade
- The cat moving through it all

**Internal Monologue**:
"I was there. In the real version, I was in his apartment. I remember the view, the layout, the way the laboratory equipment was concealed behind decorator panels. If the cat lived there, if it was part of his daily work, then it might still be there."

**Hope Emerging**: First concrete lead

**Practical Concern Rising**: But how to get inside?

**Ends With**: Wait - there's more in the memory...

---

### Beat 6: Memory Recall - The Access Codes (Est. 100-150 words)
**Type**: Solution Completion
**Purpose**: Make the plan feasible

**Action**:
Another detail surfaces from the real memory. Reginald gave her access codes - why? Perhaps trust, perhaps planning, perhaps he sensed danger.

**Memory Fragment**:
- Reginald's voice: "If anything happens..."
- A security code: #89047-RT-6824
- Biometric backup protocol
- He wanted her to be able to access the lab

**Internal Monologue**:
"He knew. Some part of him knew he was in danger, knew that his research might need protection, knew that someone he trusted might need to retrieve it. So he gave me access - not just to the apartment, but to the laboratory space inside. He made sure I could get in if I needed to."

**Realization Layering**:
- The codes are another thing the corporation doesn't know she has
- Reginald protected his research even in death
- Small act of rebellion/insurance

**Emotional Complexity**:
- Gratitude toward dead man
- Grief renewed (he trusted her, she killed him)
- Determination (she can honor that trust now)

**Ends With**: She knows what she has to do

---

### Beat 7: Decision - Retrieval Mission (Est. 100-150 words)
**Type**: Decision/Closing
**Purpose**: Transition to action, setup next scene

**Action**:
Alexa makes the decision. She will return to Golden Terrace, use Reginald's codes, break into his apartment, and retrieve the cat.

**Decision Factors**:
1. **Evidence**: Cat proves research existed
2. **Redemption**: Saving something Reginald cared about
3. **Leverage**: Physical proof against the corporation
4. **Guilt**: She owes this to Reginald

**Internal Monologue** (closing):
"The corporation took my memories, used me to kill a good man, and tried to erase all evidence of his work. But they made a mistake. They left the cat out of my false memory because the cat is real, testable, undeniable proof. And they left me with access codes because they didn't know Reginald had given them to me. I'm going back to Golden Terrace. I'm getting into that apartment. And I'm retrieving that silver-gray cat with green eyes - the only witness besides me who knows what Reginald Havenford really died for."

**Mood Shift**: From analytical to determined

**Physical Action**:
- Closes data screen
- Stands up
- Begins preparing for the mission

**Timeline Check**:
- Night of Day 2 / early morning Day 3
- Few hours until dawn
- Security lighter at night? Or heavier?
- Decision: Go now, before daylight

**Hook for Next Scene**:
Alexa heading toward Golden Terrace, codes memorized, plan forming. But infiltrating a luxury tower at 890th level won't be simple - especially for someone the corporation might be watching.

**Final Image**: Alexa moving through pre-dawn darkness toward the elevator shafts, rising toward the Golden Terrace where a silver-gray cat waits, unknowing, carrying proof in its cells.

## DEDUCTIVE LOGIC STRUCTURE

### Full Logical Chain (for reference)

```
OBSERVATION: Cat present in real memory, absent in false memory

QUESTION: Why the discrepancy?

ANALYSIS:
├─ False memory = hedonist Reginald (no research)
├─ Real memory = researcher Reginald (with research)
└─ Cat associated with research activities

QUESTION: Why specifically remove the cat?

HYPOTHESES:
├─ Random omission → REJECTED (false memory too careful)
├─ Doesn't fit narrative → WEAK (pets common for wealthy)
└─ Cat is evidence → STRONG (research animal = biological proof)

DEDUCTION:
├─ Research animals carry evidence in biology
├─ Cat exposed to temporal experiments
├─ Evidence would be testable/verifiable
└─ Corporation wants to hide verifiable evidence

CONCLUSION: Cat is living proof of research

COROLLARY:
├─ Cat likely still alive (else why hide it?)
├─ Cat would show temporal anomalies
└─ Cat is retrievable evidence

PROBLEM: Where is the cat?

SOLUTION: Reginald's apartment, Golden Terrace, 890th level

PROBLEM: How to access?

SOLUTION: Access codes from real memory (#89047-RT-6824)

DECISION: Retrieve the cat
```

### Logical Fallacies to Avoid
- **Don't**: Jump to conclusion without intermediate steps
- **Don't**: Make assumptions without justification
- **Don't**: Miss alternative explanations
- **Do**: Show Alexa considering and rejecting weaker hypotheses
- **Do**: Build conclusion from solid premises
- **Do**: Acknowledge uncertainty where it exists
